Data collection

Breast density

Planned:
A nested case-case study with 250 sets of 2 mammagrams from CBC patients, and 500 single mammagrams from BC patients who did not develop CBC.

Questions:

  1. What to consider when selecting cases
  2. Within one cohort versus between cohorts

Risk model development

Risk model development

The model

Planned:
Fine and Gray's framework for competing risks, taking into account prophylactic mastectomy, recurrent disease and and death from other causes.
Cox and Weibull models with with extrapolation to 10-year time horizon.

Questions:
  1. How do we use the estimates from the systematic review (cause-specific hazards?) in a competing risks model?
  2. If information about specific competing risks is unavailable in a cohort, how can this be handled?

Risk model development

Incorporation of IPD

Planned:
The risk estimates gathered from the systematic review will be combined with the individual patient data from the in-house cohorts.

Questions:
  1. Which is the best method for combining this information?
  2. When in-house variables aren't usable, how do we incorporate risk-estimates?
  3. How can we judge the benefit of incorporating the estimates from the systematic review?

Risk model development

Variable selection and fit

Planned:
Reduction of overfitting by 10-fold cross-validation and bootstrap resampling. Non-linearity will be assessed using restricted cubic spline functions. Calibration and discrimination can be used to judge model performance.

Risk model development

Missing data

Planned:
Missing values will be critically assessed. Some missingness is by design, where a prognostic factor is only collected in a subset. We will select only relatively complete patients and implement multiple imputation where appropriate.

Questions:
  1. Is there a basis for considering imputation of the variables not collected at all within a cohort?
  2. In general, how do we judge and subsequently handle data disparity between cohorts?

Model validation

External validation

Planned:
External validation will be performed using cross-validation by cohort, with model building on k-1 cohorts. We will additionally assess cohort by predictior interactions to determine whether prognostic effects generalize over cohorts.
